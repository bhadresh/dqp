How does the book appear on Google Books?

We recommend that you directly contact the party that submitted the book to Google. In most cases, this is the publisher. Alongside each book included in the Google Books Partner Program, we indicate that "Pages displayed by permission of [party that submitted book]."

If you feel that you are the only party authorized to submit this book to Google Books, you can file a formal DMCA complaint by taking the steps outlined at http://www.google.com/books_dmca.html.

It appears that your title may be one of the many books that we recently added to the Google Books index in a metadata-only view. This means that users will only be searching title, author, subject and copyright information, and in some cases, tables of contents and/or a book summary.

In effect, this is like seeing a library card catalog online. If you'd like to promote your book through Google, you are welcome to open a Partner Program account and complete the easy submission process.

For in-copyright books scanned through the Library Project, users can preview only bibliographic information and a few short sentences of text around a search term, similar to what might be shown in a book review. Google Books never shows full pages of these books.

If you would like to exclude a book scanned through the Library Project from Google Books, please visit this page for complete instructions.

Only books in the public domain - books whose copyrights have expired - have the download feature available. For users in the United States, this typically means books published before 1923. For users outside the U.S., we make determinations based on appropriate local laws.

As with all of our decisions related to Google Books content, we're conservative in our reading of both copyright law and the known facts surrounding a particular book. We're looking into solutions to increase the number of books accessible in full view worldwide. If you find a "Snippet View" book and believe it should be in the public domain, please contact us.

How can we help you?

The Google Books Partner Program is a free marketing program that enables publishers and authors to make their books discoverable to Google users from around the world. When a reader finds your book on Google Books, they'll see a limited preview of your book, as well as a series of links to purchase your title at major online retailers and, if you wish, your own site.

For further information on the program, please visit our help center.

The program is open to publishers and authors of all sizes. Participants may only submit books for which they hold rights, as outlined in our Program Policies. While you're welcome to submit non-ISBN books as well as books available only in digital form, at this time we are unable to accept periodical content such as magazines or journals through the Partner Program. If you're interested in submitting this content, please let us know.

Please note that at this time, we are not able to accept books which are out of copyright. We are hoping to include books like these in Google Books through the process of scanning at our partner libraries.

If you think your application would comply with our policies, we encourage you to apply. The online review process is quick and easy, and you will not be obligated in any way. When you're ready to get started with Google Books, please visit http://books.google.com/partner.

Please visit our help center for simple information on creating your Google Books Partner account.
